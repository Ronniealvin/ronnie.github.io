Queen Elizabeth National Park

Queen Elizabeth National Park, nestled in Uganda’s Western Region, spans approximately 1,978 square kilometers. This biodiverse haven is home to tree-climbing lions, chimpanzees, and a rich array of bird species. Named after Queen Elizabeth II, the park encircles the picturesque Lake Edward, offering captivating landscapes and opportunities for birdwatching. Amidst the heart of Uganda, it stands as a vital sanctuary for wildlife and a testament to the country’s natural beauty.

Kidepo Valley National Park

Kidepo Valley National Park, nestled in Uganda’s wild frontier, spans 1,442 square kilometers in the Karamoja region of northeast Uganda. Dominated by the 2,750-meter Mount Morungole, it features rugged savannahs crisscrossed by the Kidepo and Narus rivers. This virgin wilderness hosts over 75 mammal species and 470 bird species. Wildlife enthusiasts can enjoy game viewing, nature walks, and birding.

Kibale National Park

Kibale National Park, nestled in Uganda's lush forests, spans 795 square kilometers in the Kibale region of western Uganda. Dominated by the diverse landscape, it features dense rainforests interlaced with picturesque rivers and lakes. This enchanting sanctuary hosts over 70 mammal species and 375 bird species. Nature lovers can indulge in activities such as wildlife viewing, guided forest walks, and birdwatching.
